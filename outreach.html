Science by everyone and for everyone

Women in Science

While women make up half the national workforce in the US, the gender gap in science persists. Women in STEM (science, technology, engineering, mathematics) make up less than 30% of the workforce, but science should be made by everyone and for everyone. Let us bridge this gap by starting to get informed!

Science for Everyone

We see physical phenomena everyday: the light from stars traveling throughout space towards us every night; sunlight getting diffracted by rain drops and forming rainbows; tides in the sea due to gravitational force from the moon. Anyone can learn and enjoy science! Good resources are easily found online: NASA videos, SciShow for kids, Insider Science.

Get Involved

Everyone is welcome and encouraged to get involved in local science events. On March 21st, Chile annually celebrates the Astronomy National Day with hundreds of activities, so stay tuned for the next celebration. You can even help us do science by classifying galaxies with Zooniverse or use your computer to search for gravitational waves!.
